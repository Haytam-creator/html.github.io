About Exoplanets

Exoplanets, or extrasolar planets, are planets that exist outside our solar system. They orbit stars other than the Sun.

Since the first confirmed discovery in the 1990s, thousands of exoplanets have been identified, expanding our understanding of the universe.

How They Are Discovered

Exoplanets can be discovered using several methods:

  • Transit Method: Observing a starâ€™s brightness dim as a planet passes in front of it.
  • Radial Velocity Method: Detecting the star's movement due to the gravitational pull of an orbiting planet.
  • Astrometry: Measuring the precise movements of a star to detect the gravitational influence of a planet.
  • Direct Imaging: Taking pictures of planets directly by blocking out the light from their parent stars.
